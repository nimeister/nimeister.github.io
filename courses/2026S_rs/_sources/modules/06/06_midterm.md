# 06 - Midterm Exam 

Can we use satellite surface temperature to monitor heatwaves? 

## Instruction: 
The midterm exam is like a mini-project. It is an open-book exam. 
You will need to come up a research question using satellite surface temperature and air temperature. You will implement it in GEE (satellite data processing) and Python (R) for data analysis. 

Below, I provide the datasets and example research questions:  

Research Procedure:
1. Conduct a literature review to find papers dealing with research questions using 
surface temperature and air temperature data in the US.
2. You come up a research question for your own study site
3. Extract the surface temperature from both Landsat and MODIS data on Terra and Aqua satellites

## 1. Datasets
### Satellite Surface temperature data:
Landsdat, MODIS from Terra and Aqua (two separate MODIS datasets) from GEE 

### Air temperature data 
1. National scale 
Daily temperature data from weather stations, including max, min, and average temperature data can be obtained from 
NOAA’s National Centers for Environmental Information [(NCEI)](https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/)
You can search for the station's daily min, max and average temperature data from this link: 
https://www.ncdc.noaa.gov/cdo-web/search
2. New York State
New York State Mesonet provides excellent weather [data](https://www.nysmesonet.org/). You need to submit request to get the data.  

## 2. Example research questions
1. How do they compare? Are they the same or not? If not how and when they are different?
2. Can we use the long-term MODIS data to monitor heatwaves and their frequency?
